Ramadan: The Holiest Month in Islam

Discover the meaning, values, and spiritual significance of Ramadan.

About Ramadan

Ramadan is the ninth month of the Islamic (Hijri) calendar and is considered one of the holiest months for Muslims around the world. During this sacred month, Muslims fast from dawn until sunset while dedicating more time to worship, prayer, reading the Holy Quran, and performing acts of charity.

Key Aspects of Ramadan

🌙 Fasting (Sawm)

Muslims abstain from food, drink, and negative behavior from dawn until sunset as an act of worship and self-discipline.

🤲 Blessings and Forgiveness

Ramadan is a month filled with mercy, blessings, and forgiveness. Good deeds are believed to receive multiplied rewards.

📖 Revelation of the Quran

Ramadan commemorates the revelation of the Holy Quran to Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him).

⭐ Laylat al-Qadr

A night better than a thousand months, found during the last ten nights of Ramadan.

❤️ Family & Community

Ramadan strengthens family relationships through Suhoor, Iftar, charity, and helping those in need.
